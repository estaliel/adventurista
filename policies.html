Our Policies

Every province in the Philippines' Eastern Visayas, or Region 8, has its own tourist policies to support sustainable growth, conserve cultural heritage, and safeguard the environment. An outline of each of the six provinces' tourist policies is provided below:

Leyte

Environmental Conservation:

Policies emphasize eco-tourism, protecting areas like Lake Danao and Kalanggaman Island. Strict waste management and low-impact infrastructure are encouraged.

Community Engagement:

Local communities are involved in tourism activities, ensuring they benefit directly, particularly in rural and heritage areas.

Disaster Preparedness:

Leyte integrates disaster resilience in tourism planning, given its history with typhoons. Policies focus on building robust tourism infrastructure.

Southern Leyte

Marine Conservation:

Policies emphasize eco-tourism, protecting areas like Lake Danao and Kalanggaman Island. Strict waste management and low-impact infrastructure are encouraged.

Cultural Promotion:

The province supports cultural tourism with festivals like Lubi-Lubi Festival, promoting Southern Leyte’s heritage and culture.

Agritourism:

Policies encourage agritourism, supporting local farmers and creating farm-to-table experiences for tourists.

Samar

Protected Areas Management:

Policies protect natural attractions like the Sohoton Natural Bridge National Park by regulating visitor numbers and enforcing strict environmental guidelines.

Adventure Tourism Development:

Samar is promoting adventure tourism, such as caving and river trekking. Guidelines ensure safety and minimize environmental impacts.

Local Empowerment:

Programs focus on training locals to act as tour guides and manage eco-tourism sites, providing both employment and conservation education.

Eastern Samar

Eco-Tourism Regulations:

Policies for eco-tourism are strict, especially in sites like Calicoan Island, to maintain its natural beauty. There are rules on limiting infrastructure and waste management.

Cultural Heritage Preservation:

The province promotes cultural tourism through events that showcase Eastern Samar’s traditions and protect local crafts and heritage.

Surfing Tourism:

Guiuan, known for surfing, has policies ensuring that tourism development around surf spots respects the environment and supports local surf communities.

Northern Samar

Infrastructure Improvement:

Policies support improving road access to key sites while ensuring that these upgrades are eco-friendly to maintain the area’s natural beauty.

Community-Driven Tourism:

Local communities play a role in managing tourism, particularly at attractions like Biri Rock Formations, where policies emphasize sustainable practices.

Wildlife Protection:

Northern Samar’s policies also focus on protecting wildlife habitats, with regulated tourism in areas with significant biodiversity.

Biliran

Health and Safety in Hot Springs and Waterfalls:

Known for its natural hot springs and waterfalls, Biliran enforces policies that protect these sites from overuse and contamination.

Sustainable Island Tourism:

As an island province, Biliran has policies to protect coastal ecosystems and coral reefs, limiting tourism activities that could harm marine life.

Agricultural Tourism Support:

The province encourages tourists to engage in agricultural experiences, supporting the local farming community while promoting rural tourism.

Destination management

Region 8, often referred to as the Eastern Visayas, destination management is centered on striking a balance between resilience and sustainability and tourism growth. This area, which consists of the nearby islands of Leyte, Samar, and Biliran, is renowned for its historical landmarks, stunning beaches, natural beauty, and rich cultural legacy. The following are some essential components of regional destination management:

Tourism Development and Promotion

Cultural and Historical Sites: Region 8 highlights its role in Philippine history, such as the Leyte Landing Memorial in Palo, Leyte, and Guiuan's historical significance. Destination management promotes these sites through collaborations with local government units (LGUs) and cultural institutions.

Natural Attractions: Samar's caves, the Kalanggaman Island in Leyte, and Biliran’s waterfalls attract nature enthusiasts. These sites are marketed for eco-tourism, focusing on minimal environmental impact while generating income for the local communities.

Sustainable Tourism Initiatives

The region emphasizes eco-tourism and community-based tourism to support local livelihoods while preserving nature. Local communities often lead activities such as guiding tours in natural attractions, particularly in the caves and waterfalls.

There are ongoing efforts to educate both visitors and residents on sustainable tourism practices, which include waste management, conservation, and respect for local customs and environments

Disaster Resilience and Recovery

Region 8 is frequently affected by natural disasters like typhoons, making disaster resilience a critical part of its destination management strategy. After Super Typhoon Yolanda (Haiyan) devastated the region in 2013, local and national agencies developed more robust disaster preparedness plans, infrastructure improvements, and training for communities in crisis management.

The recovery efforts included restoring tourism infrastructure, creating resilient structures, and strengthening the capacity of tourism providers to handle future risks, which are integral to sustaining tourism activities.

Infrastructure and Accessibility

Infrastructure improvements are essential for enhancing tourism in Region 8. Government programs focus on upgrading roads, airports, and ports to improve accessibility to popular destinations like Kalanggaman Island and the caves of Samar.

The government collaborates with private entities for transportation and accommodation development to make remote areas more accessible and encourage tourism.

Heritage Conservation and Local Empowerment

Many initiatives encourage the involvement of local communities in tourism, especially for managing cultural heritage sites and natural parks. For instance, historical landmarks are often maintained through local partnerships, which also employ community members as guides.

Programs focus on preserving traditional crafts, cuisine, and festivals, allowing visitors to experience the region’s authentic culture while providing income to residents.

Partnerships and Stakeholder Engagement

Coordination among the Department of Tourism (DOT), LGUs, NGOs, and private sector entities is crucial for a holistic approach to destination management. These partnerships aim to support sustainable practices, improve visitor experiences, and address environmental challenges.

Partnerships also extend to disaster preparedness training, especially with international organizations that bring in expertise and resources to help the region adapt and thrive.
