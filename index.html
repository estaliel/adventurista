Natural Wonders

Eastern Visayas is a paradise for nature lovers, boasting a variety of breathtaking landscapes.

Mainit Hot Springs, Biliran

These hot springs, located in the municipality of Caibiran in Biliran, are a popular place to relax and rejuvenate. The hot water originates from the island's active volcanic and mountainous areas, providing a unique and therapeutic experience.

Biliran Volcano (Mt. Suiro)

Standing at 1,300 meters, this dormant volcano is Biliran province's highest peak. It offers hikers breathtaking views of the entire island and is an excellent location for camping and trekking.

Amandaraga Falls, Eastern Samar

This cascading waterfall in Lawaan, Eastern Samar, is named after the flowing waters that resemble maiden's hair (daraga means "maiden" in the local dialect). The nearby Bulu Sao River is a popular swimming and tubing destination.

Kalanggaman Island, Leyte

This pristine island is known for its white sand beaches, crystal-clear waters, and diverse marine life. It's an ideal spot for snorkelling, diving, or simply relaxing in a tropical paradise.

Lake Danao, Ormoc City, Leyte

This beautiful volcanic lake is surrounded by lush forests and has breathtaking views. It's a popular spot for kayaking, fishing, and relaxing in nature.

Rich Historical Heritage

Eastern Visayas boasts a vibrant cultural heritage, with unique traditions, festivals, and arts.

Waray People

The Waray, the indigenous people of Eastern Visayas, are well-known for their warm hospitality, strong community ties, and extensive oral tradition. They have passed down their history, values, and beliefs to future generations.

Buyogan Festival

This festival in Eastern Samar commemorates the region's rich agricultural heritage. It includes colourful floats, traditional dances, and lively music that reflect the community's pride in its agricultural bounty.

Pintados-Kasadyaan Festival

Held annually in Tacloban City, this festival commemorates the region's history and culture. It features colourful costumes, traditional dances, and lively music that capture the Waray people's vibrant spirit.

Handicrafts

Eastern Visayas is known for its skilled artisans who make beautiful and intricate handicrafts such as woven mats, baskets, and furniture. These crafts reflect the region's cultural heritage as well as its people's artistic abilities.

Famous Delicacies

Eastern Visayas is a culinary paradise, with a diverse range of delicious dishes that will tantalize your taste buds.

Binagol

This sweet and nutty Leyte delicacy is made with grated coconut, sweetened condensed milk, sugar, and mashed taro or gabi root, all steamed to perfection inside a coconut shell.

Waray Leche Flan

This creamy and decadent treat is a unique take on a traditional Filipino dessert. It's made with fresh carabao milk and coconut milk, which gives it a distinct flavour and consistency.

Moron

This sticky rice pudding is similar to suman, but made with coconut milk and cocoa. It's soft and flavourful, with a rich cocoa taste.

Suman Biliran

This delicious suman from Biliran comes in a variety of flavours, including ti nam biran (a combination of white and brown glutinous rice), plain white suman, and suman with mango marmalade.

Humba

This savoury dish consists of pork belly cooked in soy sauce, vinegar, and brown sugar. It is a popular dish in Eastern Visayas, distinguished by its rich and flavourful flavour.
